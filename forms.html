Child Trafficking

Part of the Human Trafficking Awareness Project

Forms of Child Trafficking

Child trafficking takes many forms, often hidden behind false promises or coercion. Common types include:

  • Sexual Exploitation: Children are forced into prostitution, pornography, or sex tourism.
  • Forced Labor: Victims work in agriculture, mining, factories, or as domestic servants with no pay or rights.
  • Illegal Adoptions: Children are kidnapped or sold and trafficked across borders under false legal pretenses.

Tactics Used by Traffickers

Traffickers use a range of deceptive and violent tactics to recruit, control, and transport children:

  • Grooming: Building trust before exploiting the child.
  • False Promises: Offering jobs, education, or a better life.
  • Document Fraud: Falsifying passports and birth certificates.
  • Isolation: Moving children far from their homes to prevent escape or help.

Organized Crime and International Networks

Child trafficking is often controlled by powerful crime syndicates that operate internationally. These networks:

  • Engage in human trafficking alongside drugs and arms smuggling.
  • Have corrupt officials to facilitate illegal adoptions and transport.
  • Use digital platforms and dark web services to trade children anonymously.
