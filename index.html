About Human Trafficking

Human trafficking is a global crisis affecting millions. From this website you can learn more about
human trafficking.


Key Terms:

  • Human Trafficking:
    The exploitation of individuals through force, fraud, or coercion
  • Child Trafficking:
    Trafficking involving anyone under 18, regardless of consent.
  • Forced Labor:
    Work performed involuntarily and under threat.
  • Exploitation:
    Taking unfair advantage of someone for personal or financial gain.

The Global Reality:

An estimated 1.2 million children are trafficked every year. Children are targeted
due to their vulnerability, often ending up in forced labor, domestic servitude, armed conflict,
or the sex trade. This is not just a problem in one country — it's a global
crisis affecting millions of lives.

Example case 1

Jeffrey Epstein & Ghislaine Maxwell Ring


What happened:

Wealthy financier Epstein trafficked dozens of girls (ages 14–17) for years, aided by Maxwell.

Tactics:

Recruited vulnerable girls through fake modeling jobs and money offers.

Outcome:

Epstein died in jail (2019), Maxwell sentenced to 20 years (2022).

Example case 2

Operation Baby Broker” (2000s) – Brazil


What happened:

This was an international trafficking ring where Brazilian babies were illegally sold to families in Europe, especially Italy and the U.S.

Tactics:

Mothers were paid or tricked into giving up their babies; false paperwork made the adoptions appear legal.

Outcome:

Brazilian and international authorities arrested several lawyers, doctors, and notary officials.
